Who was Gautam Buddha?

Buddha, which in Hindu means "Enlightened One", was the name given to Siddhartha Gautama, a religious leader who lived in India, whose kindness and wisdom earned him that title. He is considered by Buddhists to be the "Supreme Buddha", the founder of Buddhism. Buddha (Siddartha Gautama) was born around the year 563 BC. C. in the locality of Kapilavastu, capital of the kingdom of Sakia, in the northern and mountainous region of India that today is part of the territory of Nepal.

Time line

  • 563 BCE: Siddhartha Gautama, future Buddha, is born in Lumbini into the royal family of the Kingdom of the Shakyas, now part of Nepal.

  • 534 BCE: Prince Siddhartha goes outside the palace for the first time and sees the Four Visions: an old man, a sick man, a dead man, and a holy man

  • 528 BCE: Siddhartha attains Enlightenment in Buddha Gaya (present-day Bodhgaya), travels to the deer park in Sarnath (near Varanasi), India, and begins expounding the Dharma.

  • 528 BCE According to legend, Trapusha and Bhallika, two merchant brothers from Okkala (present-day Yangon), offer Gautama his first meal as an enlightened Buddha. The Buddha gave eight hairs of his hair to the two brothers; the wires are taken back to Burma and stored in the Shwedagon Pagoda. Therefore, according to this myth, this was the year of construction of this pagoda.

  • 483 BCE: Gautama Buddha dies in Kusinara (now Kushinagar), India. Three months after his death, the First Buddhist Council takes place.
